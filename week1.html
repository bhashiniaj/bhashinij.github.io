Phase 1: Literature Review & Planning

Week 1: System Architecture, Battery Study & Range Anxiety

Phase: Phase 1: Literature Review & Planning

Status: ✅ Done

What We Did This Week

  • Held project kickoff meeting with Dr. Prabath J. Binduhewa and agreed on project scope.
  • Defined the five-part system architecture: PV Panel → MPPT Controller → DC-DC Boost Converter → Supercapacitor Buffer → 48V LiFePO₄ Battery Pack.
  • Researched EV battery options across different vehicle types (e-bicycle, e-scooter, three-wheeler, EV car) and selected the 48V LiFePO₄ pack (15S, 6Ah, 288Wh).
  • Studied the supercapacitor (EDLC) buffer: placed between MPPT controller and battery, it absorbs rapid current spikes from cloud-shade cycling — reducing battery stress by 20–30%.
  • Investigated Range Anxiety in solar-charged EVs and identified design strategies: correct battery sizing, always-on MPPT, supercapacitor smoothing, and optional grid backup on 48V bus.
  • Created the 15-week project timeline and Gantt chart.
  • Finalised the 15-week Gantt chart covering all project phases.

    • Project Gantt Chart

    15-week project timeline covering all phases from literature review to final hardware submission.

    Challenges & Solutions

    Finding battery comparison data specific to light EVs (e-bicycles, three-wheelers) in Sri Lanka was difficult. We built our own comparison table from multiple sources and selected based on our 48V bus voltage requirement.

    Plan for Next Week

    Focus on the solar generation side: PV panel selection, MPPT algorithm, daily energy calculations, and starting the boost converter parametric design.
