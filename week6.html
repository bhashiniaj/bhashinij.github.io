Week 6: Prototype Design Calculation, Simulation and PWM Control Study

Phase: Phase 2: Sensor Development & Simulation

Status: ✅ Done

What We Did This Week

  • Calculated boost converter design parameters for a 20W panel and a 48V battery.
  • Identified and listed all hardware components required for the physical prototype build.
  • Simulated the open-loop boost converter in PSCAD and verified output voltage of 48.4V with CCM operation confirmed.
  • Conducted a literature review on open-loop and closed-loop PWM control strategies, including PI controller-based voltage feedback for automatic duty cycle regulation.
  • Reports covering the design calculations and PWM control study.

Challenges & Solutions

It was hard to choose the right solar panel for the prototype. Sir helped us and we finalised a 20W panel with 17.93V input and 48V output.

The simulation was not boosting the voltage at all and showed only 21V. This was because a wrong comparator setting was used, and fixing it made the circuit work correctly.

The output was too high at 55V because the battery model was giving a higher voltage than expected. Replacing it with a simple DC source fixed the output to the correct 48.4V.
